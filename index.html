Types of Hacking

Ethical boundaries define the hacker's path. Understand the motives, legality, and impact of different hacking categories:

Black Hat Hacking (Illegal)

Unauthorized intrusion for personal gain, data theft, or sabotage. Black hats exploit zero-days and sell data on darknet markets — high risk, severe penalties.

White Hat Hacking (Ethical / Legal)

Authorized professionals who find vulnerabilities before adversaries. Certifications like OSCP, CEH, and bug bounty programs reward ethical disclosure. Trusted defenders of the cyber realm.

Grey Hat (Mixed)

Operates between both worlds — often finds flaws without permission but discloses them, sometimes for recognition. Legal gray area, but essential for awareness.
